About Extinct Fishes

It's no small matter to declare a species of fish extinct: after all, the oceans are vast and deep.


Fish are remarkable creatures that inhabit a vast range of aquatic environments, from the depths of the ocean to freshwater rivers and lakes. Beyond their value as a food source, fish play a crucial role in ecosystems and the health of our planet.

Fish are integral components of aquatic food webs. They occupy various niches, from herbivores to carnivores, and help regulate populations of prey species. Their predation keeps ecosystem balances in check, preventing the overpopulation of certain species and maintaining biodiversity.Fish also contribute to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems. When they feed on smaller organisms, they excrete nutrients as waste. These nutrients, including nitrogen and phosphorus, become available to other organisms and aquatic plants, promoting the growth of primary producers and the overall health of the ecosystem.


The ecological importance of fish extends beyond local ecosystems. Oceanic fish, such as tuna and salmon, undertake long migrations that can span thousands of miles. These migrations have far-reaching effects on marine food webs and nutrient cycling, influencing ecosystems worldwide.Fish are not only a staple in our diets but also vital components of ecosystems across the globe. Their ecological importance touches on biodiversity, nutrient cycling, environmental monitoring, and even global-scale processes. As we recognize the value of fish in maintaining the health of our planet, we must prioritize sustainable practices and conservation efforts to ensure their continued presence in our waters.
